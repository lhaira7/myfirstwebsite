DEPARTMENTAL WEEK BLOG

SEAIDITE DAY AND NIGHT EXPERIENCE

"Unlocking the flairs of an undivided realm"
October 14-18, 2019

Once again the Dragons have risen and the week-long celebration has taken place last October 14-18, 2019. For this year, SEAIDITE was divided into three realms: Land of Amusement, Land of Snowflakes, and the Land of Flowers.


TEAM AMUSEMENT DAY

Google seminar, Quiz Show, and Prayer Vigil
October 16, 2019

The third day of the SEAIDITE week celebration, marks the day our team (Amusement) which comprises the group of students from the three departments: Electrical Engineering, Electronics and Communication Engineering, Computer Engineering and Information Technology conducted a GOOGLE seminar at the Bulwagang Teodulfo Domingo building (New EMC) from 8:15 to 9:45 in the morning.

It is such a great privilege to be part of the Developer Student Club and this seminar is so wonderful as Mr. Pattaguan shares his experience when he became an ambassador of Google Developer in our school. Following this event is quizzes show between the different courses in the team where the group of IT students reaps first place, I’m very proud of it

In the afternoon at 1:30, the ECE students together with the different courses in the team had a prayer vigil to give support on the takers of the coming ECE board exam.


LAST DAY OF SEAIDITE WEEK

A day to remember!
October 18, 2019

The last day of the first SEAITE Week with the theme “Unlocking the flairs of an undivided realm,” and the Land of Snowflakes emerged as the champion.
Vice President for Academics, Dr. Emmanuel James P. Pattaguan, opened the program with the announcement of the new name of the department; formerly SEAIDITE and now the School of Engineering, Architecture, and Information Technology Education (SEAITE). Moreover, he reminded the faculty and students that although they are divided into three teams, they still belong to one department. He then left the crowd with a challenge to keep their throne in the coming foundation day.
The night continued with the different contested events: Festival of Lights, Power Duo, Dance Duo, Total Performer, Tawag ng Uwian, Boyband Battle, Twist or Shout, World of Dance, and Couture.

COURSE CONTENT
Introduction to Computing


This course covers various aspects of computer usage in the social sciences.
I've gain a lot of knowledge in this subject particularly the usage of basic microsoft offices such as excel and powerpoint and also the parts and functions of computers.

Instructor: Ms. Diane Jenalyn Datul
Computer Programming 1

This course gives students the conceptual background necessary to understand and construct programs, including the ability to specify computations, understand evaluation models, and utilize major constructs such as functions and procedures, data storage, conditionals, recursion and looping.

Instructor: Mrs. Lily Ann Dela Cruz
Discrete Math

The purpose of this course is to understand and use (abstract) discrete structures that are backbones of computer science. In particular, this class is meant to introduce logic, proofs, sets, relations, functions, counting, and probability, with an emphasis on applications in computer science.

Instructor: Mrs Lily Ann Dela Cruz
Presentation Skills

This course helps participants discover how to craft presentations around essential objectives, present key concepts and ideas with power and enthusiasm, design and present effective visuals, and employ techniques for polishing and mastering presentation delivery.

Instructor: Mrs. Marife Tibule
Computer Programming 2

The course teaches students to design and implement computer-based solutions to problems. Though it was hard to understand it was very challenging for us to really study it not just in the classroom or in the laboratory but also in our houses.

Instructor: Mrs. Lily Ann Dela Cruz
Introduction to Human-Computer Interaction

HCI is an interdisciplinary field that integrates theories and methodologies from computer science, psychology, design, and many other areas. ... The central focus of the course is a semester-long team project, in which students will design, implement and evaluate a user interface.

Instructor: Mr. Benedict Sy
Object-Oriented Programming

This course emphasizes current techniques in object oriented design, analysis, and programming. Such topics as encapsulation, information hiding, inheritance and polymorphism are studied in class assignments and programming exercises.

Instructor: Mrs. Lily Ann Dela Cruz
Data Structures and Algorithms

The course focuses on basic and essential topics in data structures, including array-based lists, linked lists, skiplists, hash tables, recursion, binary trees, scapegoat trees, red–black trees, heaps, sorting algorithms, graphs, and binary trie.

Instructor: Ms. Diane Jenalyn Datul
Web Systems and Technologies 1

This course is an in-depth study of the Internet and World Wide Web. It covers web technologies, information architecture, digital media, web development, vulnerabilities, social software, HTML/XHTML, Cascading Style Sheets, and JavaScript code. Extensive laboratory exercises are expected.

Instructor: Ms. Diane Jenalyn Datul
